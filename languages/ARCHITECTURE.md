## LANGUAGE DEPENDENCY GRAPH

The 40 languages form a directed acyclic dependency graph:

```
CPL-L (foundation — depends on nothing)
  ├── CPL-C (extends CPL-L with contracts)
  │   ├── OCL (organism charters, bounded by CPL-C)
  │   ├── BCL (business contracts)
  │   └── REL (trust requires contract framework)
  ├── CPL-P (executes CPL-L)
  │   ├── WFL (work flows are CPL-P pipelines)
  │   └── SCL (scheduling is CPL-P orchestration)
  ├── CDL (doctrine aligns with constitution)
  │   ├── CIL (monologue reflects doctrine)
  │   ├── PIL (psyche shaped by doctrine)
  │   ├── SIL (identity anchored in doctrine)
  │   └── EDL (education doctrine)
  ├── ECL (compliance enforces law)
  └── RSL (realms governed by law)

TPL (communication — independent foundation)
  ├── HCL (environment description via TPL)
  └── MML (metrics transmitted via TPL)

TIL (temporal — archives everything)
STL (story — narrates TIL data)
MYL (myth — gives meaning to STL)
SYM (symbol — visual form of MYL)

LML (meta — defines all languages)
UEL (evolution — evolves everything including LML)

CHL (chaos — handles what other languages can't)
ERR (error — narrates failures)
FRL (fringe — explores beyond all boundaries)
```
